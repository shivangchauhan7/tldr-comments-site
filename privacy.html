Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 8, 2026

What TLDR Comments Does

TLDR Comments is a Chrome extension that summarizes comment sections on websites using AI. When you click the extension icon, it reads the comments on the current page and sends them to your chosen AI provider for summarization.

Data We Collect

None. We do not collect, store, or transmit any user data. Period.

How Your Data Flows

  • Comment text is read from the current webpage by the extension running locally in your browser
  • The comment text is sent directly from your browser to your chosen AI provider (Google Gemini, OpenAI, or Anthropic Claude)
  • The AI provider returns a summary, which is displayed in the extension popup
  • No data passes through any server owned or operated by TLDR Comments

What’s Stored Locally

The following data is stored locally on your device using Chrome’s built-in storage (chrome.storage.local):

  • Your API key for your chosen AI provider
  • Cached summaries for pages you’ve previously summarized (for faster loading on revisit)
  • Your extension preferences (summary style, theme, etc.)

This data never leaves your device. It is never sent to us or any third party.

Third-Party Services

When you use TLDR Comments, the comment text from the page is sent to one of these AI providers based on your selection:

  • Google Gemini — Google’s privacy policy
  • OpenAI — OpenAI’s privacy policy
  • Anthropic Claude — Anthropic’s privacy policy

We encourage you to review your chosen provider’s privacy policy. We have no control over how they handle data sent to their APIs.

Analytics & Tracking

TLDR Comments does not include any analytics, tracking pixels, telemetry, or data collection of any kind. We don’t know who you are, what pages you visit, or what comments you summarize.

Cookies

TLDR Comments does not use cookies.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this privacy policy, we will update this page. Since we don’t collect any data, we don’t expect this to change.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, open an issue on our GitHub repository.
